and it&#8217;s not that I just like old stuff, I find old stuff to be better in certain ways &#8211; so much so that in many situations I&#8217;d pick something older than a new off the shelf product.

Just today I bought an X201 ThinkPad &#8211; firstly because it was a very good price, but most of all because it&#8217;s an older ThinkPad and these things have a certain reputation about being tough as nails. Up until a few weeks ago my main laptop was a X240 ThinkPad &#8211; and that laptop was/is great, despite the purists not enjoying the touchpad buttons, it&#8217;s a really great laptop that&#8217;s more than capable to keep up with recently launched laptops in terms of looks and performance. Well that laptop is now living a cozy life with my girlfriend, so I was in the search for the next laptop I&#8217;ll abuse for a while.

I looked at KDE Slimbooks, Macbook Airs (they really look nice), some Acers and other normal things, but I wasn&#8217;t really willing to hand over the money for any of those, it didn&#8217;t seem like it&#8217;s worth the hassle to pay so much for a machine that&#8217;s a pain to upgrade, and possibly flimsy as hell. Luckily the opportunity arose that I could buy this X201 from work so my search suddenly came to a halt, and I was set on getting it. Didn&#8217;t really care about the specs, at this point in time anything you buy will be capable of doing basically anything you need to do &#8211; so buying a new laptop isn&#8217;t the best idea generally speaking. This thing has an i5 from the 1st generation and come tomorrow it&#8217;ll also have a 240GB SSD as well. You can watch movies and you can work on it, that&#8217;s everything you need.

Now, on to why I like this laptop. It isn&#8217;t made to be thin, isn&#8217;t made to be light, so really it&#8217;s made to be useful and to get shit done. The keyboard isn&#8217;t some slim profile butterfly force feedback insular glowstick piece of crap, it&#8217;s a normal keyboard, closer to the one you have on your desktop right now &#8211; or quite possibly a lot better than it, I know it&#8217;s better than the keyboard I have at the workplace. The screen is small and not quite 16:9 because it was a better time, back when screen real estate wasn&#8217;t meant to accommodate movies, and because of the combination between a small screen and a 4:3(?) screen ratio &#8211; it&#8217;s extremely efficient and you can basically use this anywhere without pulling out a 17&#8243; monster of a laptop to quickly write something or remote into another PC. It feels sturdy, I can find parts for it and I can replace basically anything with a Phillips screwdriver and some patience. Now go out there and look for X201s and enjoy the 4:3 life.

Initially I really wanted to say this about cars as well, but I&#8217;m not sure I can objectively say that. What I can say is you can fix your car if it&#8217;s a bit older than you could your brand new G20 BMW.

&nbsp;

So yeah, you don&#8217;t need a lot nowadays, basically everything has more power than you could really need in day to day tasks.
