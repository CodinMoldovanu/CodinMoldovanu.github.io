Some words before I actually get to the point of the post: some rules may be bad and useless and without any base on facts, but they&#8217;re still laws and some of them carry punishments &#8211; so we&#8217;re pretty much forced to respect them despite how bad some of them may be. It would be nice if the state would respect them as well.

### First order of business &#8211; CCR (The Constitutional Court of Romania) and piracy.

Some time ago, when I was trying to decide on a PHP framework to learn I was looking for different books and anything related to CodeIgniter and Laravel (the latter won in my book) and at one point I stumbled upon the link presented in the image below. I obviously took a screenshot and continued my search, forgetting about the incident for some time. Two years later I found the screenshot and curiosity lead me to try accessing it again, and guess what? It&#8217;s still there. Still infringing copyright law and the literal first page of the actual pdf file.<!--more-->

Now, I&#8217;ve attached all the screenshots necessary, but I really want to know some things like: did the person who put the pdf file there actually needed it to build the website for such a high placed justice institution?

Oh look, someone on StackOverflow was handed a link towards ccr.ro for the sole purpose of illegally downloading a book in PDF form. As you can see I&#8217;m very succinct, but the images do speak for themselves.

I became annoyed and then tried to find something else that&#8217;s not in order with the website, and lo and behold: they do not have the cookie notice (required by .ro and .eu law) and they store a cookie with some data. This might seem something very small, but companies can end up being fined and paying something like 5% of their annual turnover because of the lack of the cookie notice banner, terms & conditions or privacy policy.

There&#8217;s one more website that&#8217;s on my nerves that was made with public money as well, Romania.travel, which is supposed to be a travel portal for Romania, and it&#8217;s honestly not that bad.

It&#8217;s still missing the cookie notice, privacy policy and terms&conditions, but it looks decent at least. What&#8217;s the problem? Well, the feedback form isn&#8217;t working, but it was never really meant to work. There&#8217;s no listener to the button because there&#8217;s no id to the button, there are no other scripts there, and the button is outside the form tags, form tags that have no action set, no method or anything else set.
